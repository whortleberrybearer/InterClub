# Inter Club
## Overview

Inter Club is a running competition that takes place every summer.  They 

It consists of two series:
- Inter Club Road Grand Prix  referred to as the Inter Club)
- Inter Club Fell Championship (referred to as the Fell Championship)

Each series is contested by 7 clubs:
- Blackpool, Wyre & Fylde AC
- Chorley Athletic & Triathlon Club
- Lytham St. Annes RR
- Preston Harriers
- Red Rose RR
- Thornton Cleveleys RC
- Wesham RR

Other clubs have competed in the past but have since folded or merged with other clubs (resulting in clubs changing names).

## Inter Club Road Grand Prix

The original Inter Club series started in 1985 with 3 clubs.  The races are run in a spirit of fun, as well as being quite competitive.

Each member club hosts a road race between 4 and 5 miles and are free to enter for club members.  Previously guest runners were allowed to compete, but are no longer permitted due to increased number of competitors.   The minimum age to run is 15 on race day.  Headphones are not permitted in any race.

Following the race the host club provides a buffet for all competitors.

In 2020, the series was renamed "The David Wood Inter Club Series" in his memory.

## Individual Scoring

Individual scores are based of the runners finish position in each race.  These are broken down into sex and age categories, e.g.
- Male - All male runners
- Female Vet 35 (FV35) - Females between 35 and 39
- Male Vet 40 (MV$) - Males between 40 and 44

Sex: Male (M), Female (F)
Age Category: Junior (JM, JF), Senior, Vet 35 (V35 - female only), Vet 40 (V40 - both sexes), and increments of 5 upward to V100

Each series has different scoring rules and categories have changed over the years, including the method of scoring them.

## Team Scoring

Team scoring works similar to individual scoring, but individual runner positions are grouped together to form teams.

Teams are split into by sex and age categories and consist of a specific number of runners, e.g.
- Open - 10 runners, any sex or age category
- Female - 5 females runners, any age category
- Vets - 4 runners, only females over 35 and men over 40
- Vet50 - 3 runners, either sex over 50

Each series has different scoring rules and categories have changed over the years including the method of scoring them.

## Awards

Each year team and individual awards are available across various categories and differ between series.  These are based on the team and individual scores calculated after each race.

The number of categories and prizes awarded has changed over the years, along with the rules used to award them.
